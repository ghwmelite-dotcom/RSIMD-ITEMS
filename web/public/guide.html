Getting Started

Registration

New users can self-register with an OHCS email address. Only @ohcs.gov.gh domain emails are accepted. Authentication uses a 4–6 digit PIN (not a password).

1
Open the System
Navigate to the RSIMD-ITEMS URL. The system boot sequence will play, then the login screen appears.
2
Click "Register" Tab
Switch from the Sign In tab to the Register tab at the top of the form.
3
Fill Your Details
Enter your full name, OHCS email (must end with @ohcs.gov.gh), and create a 4–6 digit PIN. Phone number is optional.
4
Create Account
Click "Create Account". You will be automatically logged in and taken to the Dashboard.
⚠
Remember your PIN. There is no PIN recovery yet. If you forget it, contact the System Administrator to reset it from the Admin panel.

Signing In

Enter your OHCS email and 4–6 digit PIN on the Sign In tab. New accounts start as Technician role. An admin can upgrade you to Lead or Admin.

Installing as App (PWA)

On mobile, you may see an "Install RSIMD-ITEMS" banner. Tap Install to add it to your home screen for quick access — it works like a native app with offline capability.

03
Equipment Registry

Registering Equipment

1
Click "Register Equipment"
Opens a form to add a new device to the registry.
2
Fill Device Details
Select type (desktop, laptop, printer, etc.), enter make/model, processor, serial number, OS version, and processor generation (for Win11 readiness tracking). Select the directorate/unit location and room number.
3
Asset Tag Generated
The system auto-generates a unique asset tag (e.g., OHCS-DES-A1B2C) and a QR code you can print and stick on the device.

Health Scores

Every device gets a 0–100 health score computed from:

  • Age of the device (years since installation)
  • Number of corrective/emergency maintenance logs
  • Time since last maintenance
  • Windows 10 EOL penalty (−20 points)
  • Legacy processor penalty (Pentium/Celeron: −10 points)

Win11 Readiness Badges

Each device displays a badge based on its processor generation and OS version:

  • Ready — Already running Windows 11
  • Can Upgrade — Hardware supports Win11 (8th gen+ Intel or Zen 2+ AMD)
  • Cannot Upgrade — Hardware does not meet Win11 requirements

Aging Fleet Report

ℹ
Click "Fleet Report" on the Equipment page to see age distribution, OS breakdown, and a list of all equipment flagged for replacement — exportable as CSV for procurement requests.

Bulk QR Labels

Select multiple devices using checkboxes, then click "Print QR" to generate an A4-formatted label sheet with QR codes, asset tags, and room numbers. Print and attach to physical equipment.

Bulk Import (Admin)

In the Admin panel's Bulk Import tab, upload a CSV file to register multiple devices at once. The CSV should have columns: type, make, model, processor, serial_number, org_entity_id, room_number, os_version, processor_gen.

04
Field Log

The online fillable field log form at /field-log lets you record maintenance visits in a structured checklist format. Access it from the Dashboard's "Field Log" button.

How It Works

1
Select Location
Choose the directorate/unit and room number you are visiting. The technician name is auto-filled from your account.
2
Tick Issue Checklist
All 14 maintenance categories appear as checkboxes. Tick each category you worked on. For each checked item, enter the count of issues found and a brief resolution note.
3
Complete Additional Fields
Fill in the equipment inspection table (optional), additional notes, and any challenges encountered during the visit.
4
Submit All at Once
Each checked category creates a separate maintenance log automatically. Five checked categories = five logs created in one submission.
💡
One checked category = one maintenance log. 5 checked = 5 logs created automatically. This is the fastest way to record a multi-issue field visit.

Offline Support

The field log works offline — data saves to IndexedDB and syncs automatically when your connection is restored.

Printable Version

A printable paper field form is still available at /field-form. A link to it appears on the field log page for technicians who prefer paper forms.

05
Maintenance Logging

Logging an Activity

1
Click "Log Activity" or press N
Opens the maintenance log form (single-entry) from any page. This is different from the Field Log checklist — use this for individual one-off maintenance entries.
2
Select Location & Type
Choose the directorate/unit, maintenance type (routine, corrective, emergency, etc.), and category (internet, printer, etc.).
3
Describe the Work
Type a description — or tap the microphone icon to dictate using voice input. The system transcribes your speech.
4
Attach Photos (Optional)
Tap "Add Photo" to capture before/after images. Up to 3 photos per log, max 5MB each.
5
Submit
The system auto-calculates the quarter and month from the date. If you are offline, it saves locally and syncs when you reconnect.

Quick Repeat

When logging via QR scan, if the equipment has previous maintenance records, a "Repeat last" button appears. One tap fills in the previous description, resolution, category, and type — ideal for recurring issues.

Filtering Logs

Use the filter dropdowns on the Maintenance page to view logs by year, quarter, maintenance type, or location. Click "Export CSV" to download the filtered data.

06
QR Scan-to-Log

The fastest way to log maintenance — scan the QR sticker on any registered device.

1
Open Scan Page
Tap "Scan" in the bottom nav (mobile) or sidebar (desktop), or press S.
2
Point Camera at QR Code
Click "Start Scanner" and point your phone camera at the QR sticker on the equipment. The scanner reads it automatically.
3
Log Form Pre-filled
The maintenance form opens with equipment details, location, and room number already filled in. Just add description and submit.
💡
You can also access equipment directly by URL: /scan/OHCS-DES-XXXX
07
Quarterly Reports

Auto-generate the quarterly DOCX maintenance report matching OHCS's exact format — complete with AI-written narratives and data tables.

Two-Step Generation

1
Generate Preview
Select year and quarter, click "Generate Preview". The system aggregates all data and generates AI narratives (30–60 seconds). A full preview appears with all sections.
2
Review & Edit Narratives
Each section shows the AI-generated text in an editable box. Fix any errors, add context, or rewrite sections before finalizing.
3
Generate DOCX
Click "Generate DOCX" to create the final Word document with your edited narratives + data tables. Download from the reports list.

Report Format

The generated DOCX follows the official OHCS report template:

  • Bookman Old Style font throughout
  • OHCS-formatted tables with proper borders and shading
  • Table of Contents with clickable section links
  • Headers and footers with OHCS branding
  • Page numbers on every page
  • Numbered table captions (Table 1: Summary, Table 2: Routine, etc.)

Report Structure

AI-generated introduction referencing the exact quarter, year, and total maintenance count. Objectives section describes RSIMD's mandate.
Describes the 5 maintenance types used by OHCS: condition-based, routine, corrective, emergency, and predictive.
Five sub-sections (3.1–3.5) each with AI narratives and data tables. Routine and emergency include monthly breakdown tables. Corrective includes summary + directorate/room breakdown tables.
Smart-generated from data patterns: recurring issues, Windows 10 EOL risk, high-volume categories, aging fleet warnings. Not a generic template — real findings from your data.
Smart-generated recommendations: equipment replacement counts, Win11 upgrade blockers, root cause investigations. Conclusion summarizes the quarter's impact.
⚠
Only Lead and Admin roles can generate reports.

10
Offline Mode

RSIMD-ITEMS works without internet — critical for field technicians in areas with poor connectivity.

How It Works

  • Eager cache priming — all reference data (equipment, org entities, categories) is cached in IndexedDB on login
  • All GET responses cached — not just a few paths; every API read is available offline
  • Maintenance logs and field logs are saved locally and queued for sync
  • Photo queue — photos are saved as base64 offline and uploaded when connectivity returns
  • The header shows a "Offline — showing cached data from 5m ago" stale-data banner
  • When you reconnect, logs auto-sync with retry and exponential backoff (30s, 60s, 120s)
  • The submit button changes to "Save Offline" when disconnected
  • Network-first navigation — always fetches the latest app version when online
💡
Install the PWA (Add to Home Screen) for the best offline experience. The app shell loads instantly from cache even without internet.
11
Dark Mode & Theme

RSIMD-ITEMS supports both light and dark themes for comfortable viewing in any environment.

Toggling Theme

  • Click the sun/moon icon in the header to switch between light and dark mode
  • On first visit, the system respects your OS/browser preference (prefers-color-scheme)
  • Your choice is persisted in localStorage and remembered across sessions
  • All pages, modals, charts, and the Field Log form adapt to the selected theme
12
Data Export

Export your data from any table in the system for use in spreadsheets or external analysis.

CSV Export

  • Available on all data tables: equipment registry, maintenance logs, and admin tabs (workload, audit log)
  • Exports the currently filtered data — apply year, quarter, or location filters first to narrow your export
  • Click the "Export CSV" button above each table to download
  • Files open directly in Excel, Google Sheets, or LibreOffice Calc
💡
The Fleet Report also exports as CSV — useful for sending procurement replacement lists to management.
13
Keyboard Shortcuts

Desktop power users can navigate faster with these shortcuts (only active when not typing in an input field):

KeyAction
NOpen new maintenance log form (single-entry — not the Field Log checklist)
SNavigate to QR Scanner page
/Focus the global search bar
?Show keyboard shortcuts help
EscClose any open modal or dropdown
ℹ
The N shortcut opens the single-entry maintenance log modal. To access the Field Log checklist (multi-category batch form), use the "Field Log" button on the Dashboard or navigate to /field-log directly.

Global Search

The search bar in the header (press /) searches across equipment (asset tags, make, model), maintenance logs (descriptions), and org entities (names, codes). Results appear in a categorized dropdown — click any result to navigate directly.
